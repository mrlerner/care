ME/CFS Home Care — Caregiver Resources

A small set of printable forms and plain-language guides for caring for a bedbound person with severe or very-severe ME/CFS at home. Built to be usable by family and rotating aides, on paper, with no math.

⚠️ These are organizing tools and research summaries — not medical advice. A doctor and dietitian decide food amounts, fluids, and any medicine changes. Nothing here replaces clinical care.

Daily Care Forms →

Print-and-fill paper forms: food & fluid log, weight, lab/electrolyte tracker, symptom sheet, turning chart, medication record, activity log, and crash log.

Prioritized Tracking Documents →

Which forms matter most and why — a tiered list mapping the paper system to safety, daily care, and cause-and-effect (PEM) analysis.

Gentle Motivation — A Guide for Caregivers →

How to encourage without pushing. Grounded in ME/CFS pacing and energy-envelope research, for the people providing care.

Stories of Hope →

Real, specific things that helped people recover from severe ME/CFS — drawn from published recovery accounts.
