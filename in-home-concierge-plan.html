Might be helpful — prioritized

1. Intravene Wellness Therapies Using ✓

The mobile-IV service the family has actually used and recommends — the go-to for at-home fluids.

  • Every nurse is a critical-care RN (ICU/ER-trained); they only do IVs.
  • Usually same-day, typically arriving within ~60–90 minutes during standard hours.
  • About $200 per visit (starts at $169, varies by treatment). Private-pay.
  • Serves Seattle and the Eastside (Bellevue, Kirkland, Redmond, Mercer Island).

When booking, describe the patient honestly — bedbound, severe ME/CFS, recent suspected medication reaction — and ask whether they'll accept the visit or want clinician clearance first. An IV treats dehydration, not the underlying reaction.

2. MD² (MD Squared), Bellevue Intro in progress

Concierge medicine where house calls are a signature, fee-covered part of the model — the doctor gives you their cell and will come to the home, day or night (md2.com/offering). Each physician caps at ~50 families, which is what makes that access possible.

  • Eastside physicians include Dr. Shawn Lee (1231 116th Ave NE, Ste 700, Bellevue; (425) 451-3043) and Dr. Amy Markezich (former Overlake critical-care / pulmonology — a strong fit for a fragile, complex patient).
  • Private-pay, ~$24,000/year (first family member). No ME/CFS specialty — would be the generalist quarterback, not the specialist brain.

Status: a warm personal intro to MD² Bellevue (Dr. Shawn Lee) is in progress through a physician contact. Plan: wait for the intro, then follow up. To confirm: their model is built around mobile, traveling patients, so verify directly that they'll take a fully home-based patient (every visit a house call).

Pathway Geriatrics Ruled out

Referred by Discover Health. The model is right — in-home primary care, insurance-billed (Medicare / Medicaid / most plans), with a 24/7 caregiver phone line — but two dealbreakers:

  • They only serve licensed Adult Family Homes & Assisted Living Facilities, not private homes (their site: "We currently limit our services to Adult Family Homes or Assisted Living Facilities").
  • They're geriatric / dementia-focused and NP-led.

Takeaway: the right category to keep hunting is home-based primary care that visits private homes and isn't age-restricted.
