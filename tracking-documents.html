Daily Care Forms

Simple forms for the patient's caregivers. Fill them in by hand. Each form tells you what to do — there is no math, and you can't go wrong by writing things down.

⚠️ These are organizing forms — not medical advice. The doctor and dietitian decide the patient's food amounts and any medicine changes. Important: do not increase food amounts quickly — the doctor sets how fast to add calories.

✅ How much did the patient take?

¾ or more = good About half = keep an eye out Half or less = tell the nurse
  • 0 / ¼ / ½ / ¾ / all = none / quarter / half / three-quarters / all
  • Write what the patient took, not what you gave.
  • Half or less, several feeds in a row → tell the family or the nurse.

⏰ Falling behind? Check by mid-afternoon

  • Add up the totals at 3 pm.
  • Has the patient had less than about half the daily goal? Plan extra feeds before bed.
  • Don't wait until night to notice the patient is behind.

💧 Pee colour guide

Pale yellow — drinking enough
Light yellow — OK
Dark yellow — give more to drink
Orange — give more to drink, tell nurse
Brown — tell the nurse right away

🦠 Keeping food safe

  • 2-hour rule: don't leave food out at room temperature more than 2 hours (dairy blends, Ensure).
  • 1 hour if the food is warm.
  • Opened Ensure: use or put in the fridge within 4 hours; throw away after 2 days in the fridge.
  • Never serve food the patient already ate from — throw the rest away.
  • Make small portions. One portion = one use. Start a timer when food leaves the fridge or is warmed.

📞 When to call the nurse or doctor

  • the patient is peeing much less, or the pee is very dark.
  • the patient keeps taking half or less of feeds, or pees out more than they drink.
  • The 3 purple blood numbers are dropping.
  • Confusion, racing heart, trouble breathing, new swelling, or throwing up → call right away.
Why this matters: when everyone measures and writes things the same way, the notes can be trusted. That helps the nurse and doctor make good decisions for the patient.
